About the Work

Tantum Ergo is a setting of the final two verses of the Latin hymn "Pange Lingua," traditionally sung during Benediction of the Blessed Sacrament and other Eucharistic celebrations.

Text & Translation

Latin Text

Tantum ergo Sacramentum
Veneremur cernui:
Et antiquum documentum
Novo cedat ritui:
Praestet fides supplementum
Sensuum defectui.

Genitori, Genitoque
Laus et iubilatio,
Salus, honor, virtus quoque
Sit et benedictio:
Procedenti ab utroque
Compar sit laudatio. Amen.

English Translation

Therefore, so great a Sacrament
Let us venerate with heads bowed
And let the old practice
Give way to the new rite
Let faith provide a supplement
For the failure of the senses.

To the Begetter and the Begotten
Be praise and jubilation,
Hail, honour, virtue also,
And blessing too
To the One proceeding from Both
Let there be equal praise. Amen.
